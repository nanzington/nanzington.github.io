Foreword and Forewarned

I'm just going to apologize here real quick for the fact that this "website" will almost certainly look absolutely horrible. Graphic design has never been my thing, which is why I use SadConsole. I've been using SadConsole for a few years now, and I wouldn't say I'm an expert by any means, but I've been around the block a few times (then forgot, then around the block a few more times retreading ground). At least as of the time of writing this I'm in the SadConsole discord server, where I see a lot of frequently recurring questions or desire for tutorials from new people who join.

The second warning is that this isn't really a tutorial for people completely new to programming, and hopefully you're coming in here with a decent grasp of programming as a whole and ideally at least a basic grasp on C#, which is of course what we'll be using. I'll try to explain a lot of things as we go, and we'll do things incrementally, but this is not the tutorial for you if you can't write some basic programs already.

The final warning I want to issue before we get into things is that my code is often unconventional, inefficient, and frequently not the way you should be doing things. I'll try to explain why I choose to do things the way I do them, but my sole goal is code that does what I need it to do in the moment that I write it. I may (and often do) later go back and realize I missed something obvious, and fix or optimize it then. If you have issues while following this tutorial you may be able to get help in the discord, just know that the first reaction before you get help will probably be "why on earth did you do it like that".
